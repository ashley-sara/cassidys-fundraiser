Her Journey

Cassidy's Story

Where It Began

Cassidy's path to Child Life didn't start in a classroom. It started in a hospital bed.

An accident in eighth grade caused a traumatic brain injury and years of chronic illness. As a teenager, she spent long days in children's hospitals searching for answers.

During those days, Child Life Specialists brought her paint and canvases. Stress balls during hard procedures. The next book in her favorite series. They even advocated for her exhausted mom.

Those small moments of comfort changed everything.

The Climb

Now, Cassidy is working to become that person for other children and families.

She earned her B.S. in Psychology with minors in Family Services and Early Childhood Development, plus extra Child Life coursework through UC Santa Barbara.

With limited opportunities in Southern Utah, she applied three rounds in a row to earn a spot in a virtual practicum and completed 145 clinical hours. Many said it wouldn't be enough. She kept going anyway.

swipe →

The Buffalo Opportunity

For her final 600-hour internship, she applied to 32 hospitals nationwide. Three interviews. Two finals. One offer.

Golisano Children's Hospital of Buffalo selected Cassidy as their Fall 2026 Child Life Intern — a rare achievement on a first application round.

This internship is not just a move to Buffalo. It is the final step in a years-long dream.

Why She Needs Support

This opportunity came sooner than expected, before Cassidy could build savings. It was too important to walk away from.

Moving across the country comes with real costs: travel, housing, groceries, and daily needs. Your support helps carry Cassidy up this final stretch.

The Impact

Why Child Life Matters

Child Life Specialists help children and families cope with medical experiences. They use education, play, preparation, comfort, and advocacy to make hard moments feel less scary.

Cassidy knows the impact of this work because she has lived it.

She remembers what it felt like to be scared, in pain, and far from normal life. She also remembers the people who helped her feel human again.

Now, she wants to offer that same care to others.

Comfort & Advocacy

Bringing care to children who are scared and families who are overwhelmed.

Education & Preparation

Helping children understand what's happening so fear becomes less overwhelming.

Lived Experience

Bringing authentic understanding to children and families navigating medical trauma.
